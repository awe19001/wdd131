Week7

You made it HALF WAY!! Your anxiety may be starting to climb at this point. Tou are halfway through the semester and you see more mountains ahead of you. Everything you have learned will be with you for the slopes ahead. Look behind you and see what you have accomplished so far in the semester. I see great examples of your code. Take a half hour and look at some of the examples from your team members and the links I have shared from others in the class. There isn’t one way to do anything, especially in programming!The simplest line of code in JavaScript can be done in 4 ways (x = x + 1; x += 1; x++; ++x;).

There are arguments for using each one. (BTW go find some articles on x++, some interesting opinions on that simple line of code. Because of that I avoid it more and more). So, take some time to look at alternatives from your peers. I love the perspective I get from looking at other’s code. I use Edabit.com as an assignment in another class I teach. Once you complete a problem you can look at how others have solved it. Sometimes I think ‘well their way is wrong, mine is elegant’, other times I think ‘wow! What a cool approach’. Be open to new ideas which means you need to be out there looking at those new ideas.

Let’s close with an eternal perspective. One of the underlying principles in the paragraph above may not be obvious. It is revelation. Revelation usually comes after lots of hard work and pondering.

DO NOT worry about the team meeting. They are optional at this point. So just state that you attended.

Sometimes when you hit a coding brick wall it can be tempting to just start throwing code against the wall to see if anything sticks. Your anxiety and frustration levels are rising and you may eventually stumble on the lines of code you need but you may not understand why they worked. The course up to this point has been based on learning principles. Stop and think about those principles and see how they apply to your coding block. Random programming is less likely to produce functioning code than principle-based analysis. – ‘precept upon precept’ Isaiah 28:10. Take this advice from someone that has hit a few coding brick walls.
